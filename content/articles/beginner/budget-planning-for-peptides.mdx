"How much does this actually cost per month?" is the question every beginner asks once, and most people answer badly the first time. Peptide budgets span a huge range — from $20/month for a modest BPC-157 recovery protocol to $400+/month for a cutting-edge GLP-1 + growth hormone stack. This article walks through how to price things honestly before you buy.

## The cost components

A peptide protocol has four cost lines:

1. **The peptide itself** — per-vial price from your supplier.
2. **Bacteriostatic water** — ~$5–15 per 30mL vial, lasts many reconstitutions.
3. **Syringes and supplies** — insulin syringes, alcohol wipes, sharps container. ~$10–30/month.
4. **Shipping** — $15–40 per order, sometimes free over a threshold.

Most of your budget is line 1. The others are small but real.

## How to price a protocol

The useful question isn't "how much is a vial?" It's "how many days does a vial last me?"

The math:

```
vial size (mg) × 1000 = vial size in mcg
vial size in mcg ÷ daily dose (mcg) = days per vial
vial price ÷ days per vial = cost per day
cost per day × 30 = monthly cost
```

### Example: BPC-157

- 10mg vial from a reputable research vendor: ~$40
- 10mg = 10,000mcg
- Daily dose: 500mcg
- 10,000 ÷ 500 = 20 days per vial
- $40 ÷ 20 = $2/day
- **~$60/month**

### Example: Semaglutide (research chemical route)

- 10mg vial: ~$180
- 10mg = 10,000mcg
- Weekly dose (mid-protocol): 1000mcg (1mg)
- 10,000 ÷ 1000 = 10 weeks per vial
- $180 ÷ 70 days = **~$2.60/day, or ~$78/month**

### Example: Semaglutide (compounded telehealth)

- Typical range: **$200–400/month** depending on clinic and dose.
- You're paying for the prescription, the pharmacy, the physician consult, and the supply chain — not just the molecule.

### Example: GHRP/GHRH stack (CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin)

- CJC-1295 (no-DAC) 5mg vial: ~$35
- Ipamorelin 5mg vial: ~$30
- Typical dose: 100mcg of each, 1–3x/day
- At 2x/day: each vial lasts ~25 days
- Combined: **~$80/month**

## Where the hidden costs are

### Blood work

If you're running anything hormonal (GH-axis peptides long term, GLP-1s, or anything affecting metabolism) it's wise to baseline and re-test. A basic panel through a service like Quest or Labcorp direct is $50–150. Comprehensive panels run $200–400. Many people budget quarterly or bi-annually.

### Storage and supplies

A small fridge shelf is free. Insulin syringes are $20–30 for a 100-pack (plenty for a month). Alcohol wipes are cheap. A sharps container is $5–10. None of this breaks budgets, but first-time buyers often forget to include it.

### Shipping delays and customs

A seized international order is a full write-off. Factor some risk into international sourcing — or avoid it.

### Failed protocols

Not every protocol works for every person. Buying 3 vials of something that turns out to not agree with you means you've spent $120 to learn you don't respond. This is normal. Build some experimentation budget in if you can.

## Where to cut without cutting quality

**Cut:** Exotic or redundant stacks. If you're on BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, Thymosin Alpha-1, and Pentosan Polysulfate all at once because you read about each one separately, you're overspending massively. Pick one thing, run it, evaluate, then layer.

**Cut:** International shipping with marginal savings. A 15% cheaper vendor in another country who charges $40 shipping and has a 30% seizure rate is not saving you money.

**Cut:** "Premium" branded research peptides. A well-known vendor with reasonable COAs at the community-average price is usually optimal. Ultra-premium branding in this space rarely reflects better product.

**Don't cut:** COA-backed vendors for the random sketchy Instagram seller who's 40% cheaper. The entire savings evaporates if the vial is underdosed.

**Don't cut:** Bac water and clean syringes. These are the cheapest part of the budget and the highest-consequence to skimp on.

## A reasonable first-month budget

For a beginner doing a single-compound BPC-157 protocol:

- 1× 10mg BPC-157 vial: $40
- 1× 30mL bacteriostatic water: $8
- 1× 100-pack insulin syringes (29–31g, 1/2"): $25
- Alcohol wipes (box of 200): $6
- Sharps container: $8
- Shipping: $15–25

**Total first month: ~$100–120**, with supplies that carry over into months 2–3.

After that, recurring cost drops to the price of the next vial plus shipping — call it $50–60/month for a single-compound BPC-157 routine.
