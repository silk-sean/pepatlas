Sub-Q peptides go into the fat layer between skin and muscle. The trick is making sure the needle actually lands in fat, not in muscle below it. Two techniques do that job — the pinch and the tent. Which one fits depends on how much fat you have in the target site.

Most beginners pinch too little, tent too late, and end up with inconsistent absorption. This is the fix.

## What each technique does

- **Pinch** — thumb and index finger grab a vertical fold of skin-plus-fat. Lifts the fat away from the muscle into a distinct cushion the needle goes into.
- **Tent** — fingers gently lift the skin *upward* without squeezing, creating a shallow "tent" of skin-and-fat over the muscle. Same goal as the pinch, lower lift.

The goal in both cases: give the needle a layer of fat to sit in at a 45° or 90° angle without touching muscle.

## Which one for which body

| Body composition | Technique | Angle | Needle length |
|------------------|-----------|-------|---------------|
| Visible belly fat, pinchable 1"+ fold | Pinch | 90° | 5/16" (8mm) or 1/2" |
| Average, some abdominal fat, pinch gets ~1" | Pinch | 90° | 5/16" |
| Lean, hard to get a full fold | Tent + angle | 45° | 5/16" |
| Very lean, <10% body fat, minimal abdominal fat | Tent + 45° or use thigh | 45° | 5/16" only |

If you can't even get a tent and your abdomen is essentially skin-on-muscle, **switch sites.** Lean users should rotate to love handles, outer thigh, or triceps where subcutaneous fat still exists.

## Pinch technique, step by step

1. Swab the site, let dry.
2. With your non-dominant hand, pinch a vertical fold of skin and fat. Thumb and index finger, maybe middle finger behind — whatever is stable. Lift.
3. Hold the syringe like a dart in your dominant hand, needle perpendicular to the skin.
4. Insert at **90 degrees** in one smooth motion. The needle should disappear to the hub or close to it.
5. Keep pinching. Push the plunger slow — 0.3–0.5mL over 3–5 seconds.
6. Count to three after the plunger bottoms out.
7. Pull straight out at the same angle you went in.
8. Release the pinch.
9. Press gently with a clean tissue. Don't rub.

## Tent technique, step by step

1. Swab the site, let dry.
2. Gently lift the skin with your non-dominant fingers — no squeezing, just a pull upward to create a small tented area.
3. Hold the syringe at a **45-degree angle** to the skin.
4. Insert smoothly. The needle should pass through skin into the shallow fat beneath, not straight down into muscle.
5. Inject slowly, same 3–5 second pace.
6. Count to three.
7. Withdraw at the same 45° angle.
8. Release the tent.
9. Press briefly with a tissue.

## Angle matters more than people realize

- **90° into a proper pinch** = needle lands in lifted fat
- **90° into flat skin on a lean body** = needle lands in muscle
- **45° into flat skin on an average body** = needle lands in fat, but shallow
- **45° with no lift on a very lean body** = needle may still hit muscle

Body comp and angle together determine depth. Don't copy a YouTube tutorial made by someone with a body type that's nothing like yours.

## Common mistakes

- **Pinching skin only, not pinching down deep enough to lift fat.** You've pinched a flap. The needle still goes straight into muscle.
- **Slamming the pin in.** Fast entry is fine, but slamming causes bounce and bruise. Firm, controlled motion.
- **Letting go of the pinch mid-injection.** The tissue settles around the needle and you lose your absorption layer. Hold it until after withdrawal.
- **Tenting when you should pinch.** Sub-Q depot absorption wants a solid fat cushion, not a thin tent. If you have a pinch available, use it.
- **Using a 5/8" or longer needle on a lean pinch.** You'll punch through the fat into muscle even with a good pinch. Stick to 5/16" for most sub-Q work.
- **Injecting too fast.** Fast injections cause stinging and sometimes back-leak out the puncture. 3–5 seconds per ~0.5mL minimum.
